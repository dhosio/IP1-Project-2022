African Leadership University (ALU)

About Me

My name is Victory Friday from Rivers State, Nigeria. I lived in Port-Harcourt, the capital city of Rivers State. My hometown is Khana LGA in Rivers State. I completed junior and senior high school in Faasaa Comprehensive College in Port-Harcourt Nigeria.

ALU Application

I heard about ALU on Twitter when Patoranking posted about it. I wanted to study at ALU because I liked the Pan-Africanism it represented and I thought it was one of the first of its kind. I started the application process late December and after passing the first hurdle - the leadership quiz, I was excited. I continued in early January and did not finish until the end of March or so. Then began the long process of waiting. I was accepted on conditional admission in late April pending my submission of a particular document. I submitted the document and was finally fully admitted in late May.

Moving

Moving to ALU for studies was an exciting, life-changing experience for me. Being someone that had never moved out of my home country before ALU, it was everything I thought it would be and more. Meeting other students was an exciting and fulfilling experience especially meeting the ones who I was already in contact with and had caught glimpses of on ALU's page.

Class Experience The classes we studied in ALU especially during our first year, were very practical and what we need for real life and in each of our careers.

Advice to prospective students I would advice prospective students to choose a course that will be beneficial to them in the future and to choose ALU because it is one of the best decisions you would make.
